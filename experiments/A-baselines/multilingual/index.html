ğŸ“ˆ Machine Learning: The Fundamentals EN

Machine Learning is a subfield of artificial intelligence that enables systems to learn and improve from experience without being explicitly programmed for each task. There are three main types of machine learning:

  • Supervised Learning: Algorithms learn from labeled data, where each training example is associated with a correct outcome. For example, a system can be trained to recognize cats in images using thousands of photos labeled as "cat" or "not cat".
  • Unsupervised Learning: Systems identify patterns in unlabeled data. Clustering and dimensionality reduction are common examples. This approach is useful when we don't have labels for our data.
  • Reinforcement Learning: Algorithms learn through trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties for their actions. This method is used in robotics, gaming, and autonomous systems.

The machine learning process involves several essential steps: data collection, preprocessing, model selection, training, evaluation, and hyperparameter tuning. Each step is crucial for obtaining a high-performing model.

Artificial neural networks are inspired by the structure of the human brain and represent the foundation of deep learning. A neural network consists of layers of interconnected nodes, called artificial neurons, which process information in a hierarchical manner.
